# Enterprise Scrum Playbook

Enterprise-grade Scrum operating standards designed for large-scale, regulated, and complex technology organizations.

This playbook defines **how Scrum actually works in enterprise environments** where predictability, governance, accountability, and executive visibility matter as much as team autonomy.

---

## Executive Context

In large enterprises, Scrum rarely fails because teams do not know the ceremonies.  
It fails because **Scrum is practiced without an operating model**.

Common failure patterns include:

- Teams following Scrum events but missing delivery commitments
- Product Owners overloaded or positioned without authority
- Scrum Masters reduced to facilitators instead of delivery leaders
- Definitions of Ready and Done treated as optional
- Leadership receiving unreliable or lagging delivery signals

This playbook establishes Scrum as a **delivery operating model**, not a lightweight team framework.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this repository is to provide **practical, reusable, enterprise-ready standards** for running Scrum with execution discipline, predictability, and continuous improvement.

The content here is designed to be:

- Adopted incrementally
- Tailored by maturity
- Applied under real enterprise constraints (compliance, scale, dependencies)

---

## Business Problems Solved

This playbook is used to correct the following enterprise delivery issues:

- Sprint commitments consistently missed
- Teams completing work that is not production-ready
- Retrospectives generating discussion but no measurable improvement
- Role confusion between Product, Engineering, and Delivery
- Inconsistent Scrum execution across teams or programs
- Leadership unable to trust delivery forecasts or progress reporting
- Scrum events devolving into status meetings

## When Scrum Should Not Be Used

Scrum may not be appropriate when:

- Work is primarily unplanned and interrupt-driven
- Flow-based Kanban systems are a better fit
- There is no empowered Product ownership
- Leadership expects predictability without governance

In these cases, alternative delivery models should be considered before forcing Scrum adoption.
